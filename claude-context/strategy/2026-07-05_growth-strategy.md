# Growth Strategy — Product, Acquisition, Visitor Intelligence & Feedback (2026-07-05)

**Status:** Active operating guide. Companion to `north-star.md` (governing product plan) and
`phase2-interview-kit.md` (interview operations). This document owns the *go-to-market* layer:
how the product develops from here, how customers are acquired, how every visit is captured and
learned from, and how early-adopter feedback drives the roadmap.

---

## 0. The honest baseline (verified against the codebase, 2026-07-05)

**The product is far ahead of its market evidence.** As of today:

| Dimension | State |
|---|---|
| Product | Complete through north-star Phase 1.5. Scorecard (free/anon/2s), cited chat, $25 report, $99 Pro, citywide Discovery, Verdict Band, i18n, mobile-audited. Lot-facts coverage ≥85% on the 100-address panel. |
| Revenue | **0 paying customers.** The $25 wedge has never been bought by a stranger. |
| Validation | Phase 2 (20 interviews) is the ACTIVE phase per north-star. The kit is fully written. **No evidence any interview has happened.** |
| Traffic | Unknown — no attribution instrumentation, so even if visitors arrive, we can't say from where. |
| Analytics | 8 first-party events (`tracking.ts` → `events` table), visitor ID + session ID, admin engagement dashboard (4 events charted). **No referrer/UTM capture, no purchase/checkout events, no visitor↔user stitching.** |
| SEO | **Effectively zero.** `index.html` has no meta description, no OG/Twitter tags, no sitemap.xml, no robots.txt; the SPA serves an empty `<div id="root">` to crawlers for every route. Google cannot read a single page of the product. |
| Email | No capture mechanism except Google OAuth sign-in. No owned communication channel. |
| Legal | No privacy policy page. Blocks expanding data capture honestly. |
| Assets already in hand | Public sample report (`/sample-report.pdf` + thumb), shared-link infrastructure, "Generated by UrbanLayer" report branding, the About page (an exceptional Show-HN-grade technical essay), the interview kit, the admin dashboard. |

**The strategic diagnosis:** the constraint is no longer the product — it's *distribution and
evidence*. Every additional product-polish week is a week the kill/validation metrics
(north-star §7) go unanswered. The next 90 days are about generating evidence at the maximum
rate: put the product in front of qualified Chicago RE professionals through multiple channels,
instrument every visit so each one teaches something, and let purchases + feature-ask tallies —
not intuition — drive product development.

**Strategy in one sentence:** *Engineering now serves the funnel; the funnel serves the
interviews and channels; the interviews and channels produce the evidence that decides what gets
built next.*

## 1. Track 1 — Product development strategy

### 1.1 The governing rules (carried forward from north-star, still binding)

1. **Build nothing feature-shaped until 3+ independent users ask for it.** The feature-ask
   ledger (§4.3) is the only queue.
2. **Exception: acquisition & measurement infrastructure.** Work that makes the funnel
   measurable, findable, or frictionless is not "features" — it is the current product priority.
3. **The kill metric stands:** if after 20 conversations + 6 weeks of real traffic nobody has
   paid, pivot — don't add features.
4. **The distraction list stands:** no geographic expansion, no enterprise/team features, no
   Spanish expansion by default, no new data sources without demand signal
   (`2026-07-02_data-expansion-candidates.md` is the parked backlog).

### 1.2 P0 — Acquisition infrastructure (build now, ~1–2 weeks of work total)

These are engineering tasks whose customer is the *funnel*, ordered by leverage:

| # | Item | Why | Sketch |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Attribution capture** | Without it, every channel experiment below is unmeasurable. Must land BEFORE any outreach/launch. | `tracking.ts`: on first event per visitor, capture `document.referrer`, UTM params, landing path; persist with visitor ID (localStorage) and send on every event batch. Backend: store on `events` rows (or a `visitors` table). |
| 2 | **Complete the funnel events** | The path visitor → address → scorecard → engage → checkout → **purchase** currently goes dark at the money step. | Add: `scorecard_view` (address + confidence + pin-resolved?), `checkout_started`, `purchase_completed` (server-side from the Stripe webhook — join via `client_reference_id`=visitor ID), `signup_completed`, `pricing_view`, `discovery_search`, `sample_report_download`. Chart the full funnel on `/admin`. |
| 3 | **Visitor↔user identity stitching** | "Which channel produced the buyer" requires connecting anonymous visitor IDs to accounts. | One event on sign-in: `identity_link {visitor_id, user_id}`. |
| 4 | **SEO foundation** | Crawlers currently see nothing. Cheap fixes first, programmatic pages later (§2.5). | `index.html`: meta description + OG/Twitter card tags (use the sample-report thumb or a hero shot). Add `robots.txt` + `sitemap.xml` (static routes now; district pages when they exist). Prerender or static-generate the landing page + marketing pages (vite-plugin or a build-time snapshot served by nginx for `/`, `/pricing`, `/about`). |
| 5 | **Privacy policy page** | Legally/ethically required before expanding capture + email; interviewees and HN will look for it. | Static `/privacy` route, footer link. Plain-language: first-party analytics, Stripe, Google OAuth, Sentry, Cloudflare, no data sales. |
| 6 | **Email capture (light)** | Email is the only owned channel; today the product can't contact anyone who didn't OAuth. | (a) Footer + post-scorecard "Get Chicago development intel — monthly" newsletter signup (a `subscribers` table; no ESP needed until there's a list). (b) "Email me this Scorecard" on ScorecardPage (send-and-store). Never gate the sample report — friction beats capture there. |
| 7 | **Segment self-identification** | THE single most valuable datum for Phase 2: who is actually showing up. | One dismissible question after first scorecard or on signup: "What best describes you?" — Developer / Architect / Attorney / Broker / Investor / Homeowner / Just curious. Store on user + fire event with answer for anonymous visitors. |
| 8 | **Per-scorecard micro-feedback** | Accuracy is the trust moat; users who catch errors must have a 1-click way to say so. | "Was this accurate?" 👍/👎 + optional "What's missing?" text at the Scorecard bottom → `events`. A 👎 with an address is a P0 data bug report. |

### 1.3 P1 — Funnel friction removal (build during the interview window)

- **Anonymous report purchase.** Today the $25 purchase requires Google sign-in — a wall at the
  exact moment of maximum conviction, on a product whose whole entry thesis is "no account
  required." Stripe Checkout collects email anyway; entitle by emailed magic link + PIN. The
  coherence audit deferred this "until conversion data shows it costs sales" — but with near-zero
  traffic that data will never arrive; during the validation window the funnel should be
  frictionless *by construction*. (Also solves email capture for buyers automatically.)
- **OG cards for shared surfaces.** A shared Scorecard/conversation link currently unfurls as
  a generic tile. Per-parcel OG meta (address, zone, verdict headline; nginx or a tiny SSR shim
  for `/scorecard` link previews) turns every share into an ad. Shared artifacts are the
  designed viral loop — make them look like it.
- **Sample report prominence.** The asset exists; make sure every pricing/report CTA surface
  offers "See a sample report" (event: `sample_report_click` already exists — watch it).

### 1.4 What NOT to build (repeat it until it sticks)

New data sources, report v7 polish, more incentive programs, zoning alerts, contractor
intelligence, more i18n, more design passes. Each is parked behind an explicit signal listed in
north-star §3. The product is *good enough to sell*. The only honest way to improve it now is to
watch someone use it.

### 1.5 Deferred-feature signal table (when demand shows up, this is the pre-agreed response)

| Signal observed | Response |
|---|---|
| 3+ interviewees ask for property *ownership* data | Price a per-lookup or Regrid license; this is Cityscape's moat — validate willingness to pay first |
| Users return to re-check the same parcels | Property tracker / watch alerts (retention + Pro justification) |
| Attorneys dominate engaged users | Pending-zoning-change extraction (Legistar PDFs) — premium feature |
| Appraisers arrive via tax-appeal searches | Package Scorecard comps + assessment history as an "appeal kit" page; seasonal campaign |
| Free users repeatedly hit Discovery teaser wall | Loosen teaser depth (top-10 → top-25) and measure Pro conversion delta |
| Report buyers don't convert to Pro after 3+ reports | Revisit Pro's value (unlimited reports isn't a recurrence engine — Discovery + alerts are) |

## 2. Track 2 — Customer acquisition strategy

Chicago-niche B2B means channels rank very differently than for a general SaaS. Ordered by
expected evidence-per-hour:

### 2.1 Tier 1 — Direct: execute Phase 2 (weeks 1–6, the spine)

The interview kit (`phase2-interview-kit.md`) is complete and production-verified. **The single
highest-priority "marketing" activity is running it**: 20 conversations (5 developers / 5
architects / 5 attorneys / 5 brokers-appraisers), 3–4/week, per the existing script, logging, and
observation checklist. Everything else in this section feeds or amplifies this.

Additions to the kit's plan:
- **Before the first formal interview:** the north-star "fastest path" step that never happened —
  send the URL cold to 5 Chicago RE acquaintances, watch the analytics, ask "would you pay $25
  for this report?"
- **Every interviewee leaves with:** a free-report code (feedback currency, §4.4) + the referral
  ask ("who else should I talk to?") + permission to email them the monthly update.
- **Interview-window instrumentation:** check the admin funnel after each interview day and
  reconcile observed behavior with the events it produced (the kit already says this — now the
  funnel events from §1.2 make it complete).

### 2.2 Tier 2 — Community seeding (continuous, ~2 contributions/week)

Where Chicago real-estate professionals and investors actually are online. The posture is
**helpful expert, not advertiser**: answer real questions with real analysis, mention the tool
when it's the honest answer, keep a link in profile/signature.

| Venue | Audience | Play |
|---|---|---|
| **BiggerPockets forums** (Illinois/Chicago sub-forums) | Small investors & developers — the primary hypothesis segment | Answer zoning/feasibility/tax questions with specifics ("that lot is RT-4, here's what that allows…"). Tool link in signature. BP threads rank on Google — answers compound. |
| **Reddit** — r/chicago (when on-topic), r/realestateinvesting, r/RealEstate | Mixed; occasional gold | Same posture. Reddit is hostile to self-promo — lead with the analysis, link only when asked or clearly warranted. |
| **LinkedIn (Jack's profile)** | Architects, attorneys, brokers — the professional segments | Build-in-public + Chicago RE content: post one parcel analysis or data finding weekly ("Every TIF district in Logan Square, mapped"). Connect with interviewees and their referrals. |
| **Facebook groups** — Chicago real-estate investor groups (several 10k+ member groups) | Small landlords/investors | Same helpful-answer play; these groups get daily "what can I do with this lot" questions. |
| **X/Twitter — Chicago urbanism & CRE** | Chicago YIMBY readership, urban-policy crowd, press | Share maps/visualizations (the product makes beautiful ones). This crowd retweets zoning content and includes the journalists in §2.4. |
| **In-person orgs sourced online** — ULI Chicago, AIA Chicago, CBA Real Property, CCIM Chicago | All four segments | Event calendars are public; attending 1–2/month doubles as interview recruiting (the kit already maps these). |

**Listening posts:** saved searches (Reddit, BP, X) for "chicago zoning", "what can I build",
"cook county assessor", "TIF district" — each hit is a chance to help in public and a live
record of how the market phrases the problem (mine these phrases for SEO + landing copy).

### 2.3 Tier 3 — Launch moments (one-time spikes, sequenced deliberately)

Do these **only after §1.2 attribution is live** — an unmeasured spike is wasted.

1. **Show HN** (highest fit): the About page is a ready-made HN essay (RAG over municipal code,
   the parcel-resolution war story, the reranker incident). Frame: "Show HN: I built an AI
   feasibility engine for every parcel in Chicago." Expected yield: feedback, credibility,
   traffic, possibly Chicago-based professionals — not direct customers. Prep: About page is the
   landing story; be present in comments all day; expect and welcome hard accuracy questions.
2. **Product Hunt** (moderate fit): lower-quality traffic for this niche, but cheap; do it in
   the same window as HN to reuse assets.
3. **Local press pitch** (high fit, harder): Crain's Chicago Business (real estate desk), The
   Real Deal Chicago, Block Club Chicago, Chicago YIMBY. Angle: "Type any Chicago address, get
   what you're allowed to build — free." A journalist demo (their own home address) is the pitch.
   Note: Streetsblog Chicago is competitor-founder territory (Steven Vance) — skip.
4. **Demo video** (prerequisite asset for all three): the north-star opportunity #3 that never
   got made. 2 minutes, screen capture: type address → Scorecard → Investigate → cited code
   answer → report. Lives on the homepage, YouTube (searchable: "chicago zoning lookup tool"),
   and in every pitch/outreach email.

### 2.4 Tier 4 — Content & SEO (compounding; start week 3–4, pays off in months)

The product's shape is *made* for programmatic SEO — free, anonymous, instant pages about
things people actually Google:

1. **Zoning-district guide pages** (first, highest intent): one prerendered page per district
   (~60: RS-1…RM-6.5, B/C/D/M classes) — "RT-4 Zoning in Chicago: What You Can Build" — generated
   from the already-deterministic `zoning_definitions.py` + zoning-cache data (FAR, height,
   setbacks, permitted uses) + FAQ + a Scorecard CTA ("check a specific address"). These match
   real queries ("rt-4 zoning chicago", "b3-2 what can I build") with weak current competition
   (the city PDF and legalese sites). Static generation at build time; add to sitemap.
2. **Cornerstone articles** (3–5, written once): "How to read your Chicago zoning district" /
   "Chicago ADU rules, explained" / "What a TIF district means for your property" / "Cook County
   property tax appeals: the data that wins them" / one real parcel tear-down analysis (the demo
   in prose). Each is also community-seeding ammunition (§2.2).
3. **Neighborhood/CA pages** (later, only if district pages get traction): 77 community-area
   development snapshots from data already in the system.
4. **The report as distribution** (already built): every purchased PDF carries the brand to a
   lender/partner/attorney. Make sure the cover/footer CTA is crisp and the URL is short.

### 2.5 Tier 5 — Paid experiments (LAST; only after the funnel converts organically)

A $200–300 Google Ads test on high-intent exact phrases ("chicago zoning lookup", "what can I
build on my lot chicago", "cook county property report") answers "what does a $25-report buyer
cost?" — but only run it once (a) attribution is live and (b) at least one organic purchase
proves the funnel *can* convert. Paid traffic into an unproven funnel measures nothing.

### 2.6 Seasonal angle — tax-appeal season

Cook County assessment appeals run on a published township calendar; appraisers and owners
surge predictably. When a township's appeal window opens: a targeted content piece + community
answers + (if funnel proven) a small paid burst on "cook county appeal comps". The Scorecard's
comps + assessment history is already the needed artifact.

## 3. Track 3 — Visitor intelligence (capture everything, honestly)

### 3.1 Principles

- **First-party first.** The existing `tracking.ts` → `events` architecture is the right spine —
  extend it rather than bolting on a third-party analytics SDK. (Exception considered below:
  session replay.)
- **Capture must never degrade UX** (fire-and-forget stays the rule) and never becomes a wall
  (no forced email gates during validation).
- **Honesty is cheap:** ship the privacy policy (§1.2#5) and describe exactly what's collected.

### 3.2 The visitor data model (target state)

Every visitor accumulates, keyed on the existing cross-session visitor ID:

| Layer | Data | Mechanism | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acquisition | First referrer, UTM set, landing path, first-seen date | tracking.ts first-touch capture | **Missing — P0** |
| Behavior | Full event stream incl. scorecard views, chats, CTA clicks, discovery searches | events table | Partial (8 events; add ~7 more, §1.2#2) |
| Intent | Addresses/areas queried (already `setAddress`), questions asked, filters used | events payloads | Partial — aggregate it in admin ("which neighborhoods is demand coming from?") |
| Identity | visitor↔user link, email, Google profile | identity_link event + OAuth + Stripe email + newsletter | Missing link + email capture |
| Segment | Self-identified role (Developer/Architect/…) | one-question ask (§1.2#7) | Missing |
| Money | checkout_started, purchase_completed, subscription events | Stripe webhook → server-side events | **Missing — the funnel's dark step** |
| Sentiment | 👍/👎 accuracy votes, "what's missing" texts | micro-feedback (§1.2#8) | Missing |

### 3.3 Admin dashboard additions

- **Funnel view:** visitors → hero_address_submit → scorecard_view → (investigate ∪ chat ∪
  report_cta) → checkout_started → purchase_completed, per period, with per-channel splits once
  attribution lands.
- **Chart the already-collected-but-uncharted events** (`scorecard_bridge_click`,
  `hero_address_submit`, `hero_librarian_click`, `sample_report_click`) — the backend allowlist
  already ingests them; this is admin-UI-only work flagged pending since 2026-06-12.
- **Segment & geography rollups:** role answers; top queried community areas/addresses.
- **Weekly digest habit** (§5) reads from this page — build for that ritual, not for dashboards'
  sake.

### 3.4 Session replay — the one third-party worth considering

Events tell you *what* happened; they can't show you *where someone stalled or what they tried
to click*. During the validation window that qualitative signal is disproportionately valuable.
Options:

- **Microsoft Clarity** (free, unlimited): fastest path; needs CSP additions + privacy-policy
  disclosure; can be removed after validation.
- **Self-hosted PostHog** (replay + funnels + first-party): philosophically cleaner, but a real
  service to run on an 8 GB box already hosting ML models — overkill now.
- **Skip replay** and rely on interviews (which ARE moderated replay) + events.

**Recommendation:** add Clarity for the validation window only, clearly disclosed; revisit
after 90 days. If that feels off-brand, the interview program covers most of the need.

### 3.5 Email as the owned channel

Sources feeding one `subscribers`/user-email pool: OAuth signups, Stripe purchase emails,
newsletter opt-ins, "email me this scorecard", interviewees (with permission). Cadence: one
monthly "Chicago development intel" email (a data finding + what shipped) — low effort, keeps
the list warm, and is the reactivation channel when tracker/alert features eventually ship. No
ESP needed below ~200 subscribers (manual BCC or a $0 tier).

## 4. Track 4 — Early-adopter feedback engine

### 4.1 In-product feedback surfaces

- **Scorecard micro-feedback** (§1.2#8) — accuracy votes with address context.
- **Persistent low-key feedback affordance** — a footer/menu "Feedback" mailto (or a 2-field
  modal → events). No Intercom-class widget; wrong ethos, wrong scale.
- **Founder-signed touchpoints:** the report PDF and the purchase receipt email should both
  carry "Built by a Chicago developer — reply with anything wrong or missing: jack@…". A $25
  buyer who found an error is the single most valuable respondent possible.
- **Post-purchase follow-up:** manual email to every report buyer 2–3 days later ("Did the
  report do its job? What was missing?"). At the current volume this is minutes/week and each
  reply is gold.

### 4.2 Online early-adopter cultivation

- Every community answer (§2.2) that lands ends with an open loop ("if you try it on your lot,
  tell me what's wrong with it — I fix data bugs same-day").
- HN/PH launch comments are a feedback harvest — log every concrete critique into the ledger.
- The listening-post searches (§2.2) double as feedback: complaints about the assessor's site or
  Cityscape's UI are feature-priority signals written by the market itself.

### 4.3 The feature-ask ledger (the 3+ rule made operational)

One file: `~/urbanlayer-interviews/feature-ledger.md` (private, next to interview logs). Every
ask from any source (interview, email, community thread, 👎 text) gets one tally line: date,
source, segment, verbatim ask. **Weekly review; anything at 3+ independent sources enters the
build queue; everything else waits.** This ledger — not taste — is the product roadmap now.

### 4.4 Feedback currency & founding users

- **Free-report codes**: for interviewees, bug reporters, and community members who engage
  seriously. Costs ~nothing, converts a stranger into someone with the deliverable in hand.
- **Founding-member offer** (when the first real users appear): lock early Pro subscribers at a
  founding price ($59–79/mo) in exchange for a monthly 20-minute call for the first 3 months.
  Cheap retention + a standing interview panel.
- **Public changelog** (`/changelog` or a section on the landing page, later): visible momentum
  is itself a community-seeding asset ("they shipped what I asked for in a week").

## 5. Operating cadence & 30/60/90 plan

### Weekly rhythm (the founder's marketing week, ~10–12h)

| Ritual | Time |
|---|---|
| 3–4 customer conversations (Phase 2 kit) | 3–4h |
| 2 community contributions (BP/Reddit/LinkedIn/FB) | 1–2h |
| 1 content unit (article, district-page batch, video segment) | 2–3h |
| Monday metrics review: funnel dashboard + feature ledger + interview rollup | 1h |
| Buyer/feedback follow-up emails | <1h |

### Days 1–30 — Instrument, then open the doors

- [ ] Ship §1.2 P0s: attribution, funnel events + admin funnel, identity stitching, meta/OG +
      sitemap + robots, privacy policy, newsletter capture, segment question, micro-feedback.
- [ ] Record the 2-minute demo video; put it on the homepage.
- [ ] Send the URL to 5 Chicago RE acquaintances; watch the funnel.
- [ ] Interviews 1–8 (recruiting via kit channels; segment-diverse weeks).
- [ ] Start the feature ledger + listening-post searches.
- [ ] First 2 cornerstone articles drafted; district-page generator scoped.

### Days 31–60 — Amplify

- [ ] Interviews 9–20; write the 5-interview rollups; first read on "which segment has the pain."
- [ ] Ship zoning-district SEO pages + sitemap; submit to Search Console.
- [ ] Show HN + Product Hunt (same window); press pitches to Crain's/TRD/Block Club/Chicago YIMBY.
- [ ] Ship P1s as friction data warrants (anonymous purchase, OG cards).
- [ ] First monthly intel email to the (small) list.

### Days 61–90 — Decide

- [ ] Paid-search test **iff** ≥1 organic purchase proved conversion.
- [ ] Build the top feature-ledger item **iff** it hit 3+ independent asks.
- [ ] **Decision gate (the north-star metrics, unchanged):**
  - **Validate:** 3+ unprompted report purchases → double down on the buyers' segment; move to
    Phase 3 (10 paying customers, $1K MRR) with channel spend focused where the buyers came from.
  - **Kill/pivot:** 20 conversations + 6 weeks of instrumented traffic + zero payments → the
    hypothesis is wrong. Pivot candidates to evaluate *at that point, with the data in hand*:
    different segment (whichever showed unexpected engagement), different wedge (Discovery-first
    for investors; appeal-season kit for appraisers), or different market posture (the About page
    proved the engineering — consulting/acqui-interest is a legitimate outcome for a showcase
    project). Do not add features to escape this gate.

### The numbers that matter (in order)

1. Unprompted report purchases (the validation metric)
2. Purchase-funnel conversion by step and by channel
3. Interviews completed / segment pain scores
4. Feature-ledger items at 3+
5. Returning visitors (visitor ID re-seen ≥7 days apart) — the earliest retention signal
6. Email list size — the owned-channel asset

Vanity numbers to ignore: total page views, HN points, follower counts.

## 6. Risks specific to this plan

- **Channel work without attribution** — mitigated by hard-sequencing §1.2 before any launch.
- **Community backlash to promotion** — mitigated by the helpful-expert posture; the product's
  free tier makes "here, just check the address" a genuinely useful reply, not an ad.
- **SEO investment outliving the validation window** — capped: district pages are generated from
  data the product already maintains (~days of work, not weeks); no content treadmill commitment
  until traffic proves out.
- **Accuracy incident in public** (an HN commenter's address resolves wrong) — the confidence
  badges, provenance labels, and honest-caveat design are the defense; respond in-thread with
  the fix. A visibly same-day data fix converts a critic.
- **Founder time** — the weekly rhythm is ~10–12h on top of engineering; the §1.4 "do not build"
  list is what pays for it.
